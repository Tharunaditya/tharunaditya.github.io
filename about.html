My Journey in Cybersecurity

What started as curiosity about "how systems break" evolved into a passion for protecting them. Today, I'm not just a cybersecurity graduate—I'm a digital guardian pursuing M.Tech in Cyber Security Systems and Networks, constantly pushing boundaries in ethical hacking, VAPT, and SOC operations.

My arsenal? Python automation scripts that hunt vulnerabilities, forensic tools that uncover digital footprints, and penetration testing frameworks like Metasploit and Burp Suite that expose weaknesses before adversaries can. But I don't stop at breaking systems—I rebuild them stronger with modern SIEM architectures like my NexGen SOC, leveraging the ELK stack and MITRE ATT&CK framework.

When I'm not in a terminal window, you'll find me designing intuitive security dashboards in Figma or developing collaborative tools like BugHunterX—a Bash-powered web penetration testing toolkit that brings real-time vulnerability assessment to teams through Discord notifications.

My philosophy? Security isn't just about walls and firewalls—it's about understanding the attacker's mind, anticipating their moves, and building resilient systems that adapt. With every project, every certification (from TCM SEC's Practical Ethical Hacking to Google Cybersecurity Professional), I'm writing my story as a defender in the endless battle for digital safety.
